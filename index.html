How it works.

Add your stuff once. Share one link. Get messages when someone wants something. That's the whole thing.

1
Add stuff by chatting with Claude

Snap a picture, tell Claude what it is and roughly what you want for it. Claude writes the title, description, and tags, and adds it to your sale. You just say yes.

2
Mark things reserved, hand stuff off

When a buyer taps Reserve on your page, you get an email. Or tell Claude. "mark the couch reserved for my neighbor". and it's done. You pick the time and take cash or Venmo.

What makes it different.

No commission

eBay takes 13%. Mercari takes 13%. Facebook takes 5%. yrdsl.app takes nothing. Your buyer pays $100, you get $100.

Claude does the writing

Photo + a sentence ("old lamp from my aunt's place, $40?") and Claude writes the title, description, and tags. You just say yes.

Buyers contact you directly

Put your email, SMS, or WhatsApp on the page. Any combination works. Your neighbors reach out on their own. No platform messages, no "are you still interested?", no inbox to manage.

Pennies a month

A normal sale costs about 10 cents a month to host. When you're done, delete it and the cost drops to zero. Sales under 50 cents a month, we skip billing entirely.

Looks good

Four themes: conservative, retro, hip, and artsy. Your sale looks like you, not like a flea-market spreadsheet.

Free & open source

You're not locked in. The whole thing is on GitHub, anyone can read the code or run their own copy.

Pennies, not percentages.

Big marketplaces take a cut of every sale. We don't. A typical yard sale costs about ten cents a month to host, and we skip billing entirely under 50 cents. When you're done, delete it and the cost drops to zero.
